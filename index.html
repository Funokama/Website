Projects

Impact of Reference-Dependence on a Sequential Contributions Game to Public Goods

Abstract: This thesis examines how reference-dependent preferences and warm-glow utility affect sequential contributions to public goods. By introducing behavioral factors and a disutility term for contribution gaps, it shows reduced free-riding, more balanced contributions, and higher total provision than in standard models.

The Wheat-Sheep Belt and the Importance of Railways and Infrastructure: Government Response to the Federation Drought

Abstract: This paper analyzes government rail and water infrastructure responses during and after Australia's Federation Drought (1895-1903). It highlights how transport systems adapted to environmental shocks and links historical policy responses to contemporary drought-resilience infrastructure management.

Trade Isolation and the Independence Debt: How Diplomatic Exclusion Created a System of Financial Colonialism in Post-Revolution Haiti (1804-1900)

Abstract: This report studies how diplomatic trade isolation and the 1825 indemnity debt shaped Haiti's post-revolution economy. It argues that restricted market access combined with debt obligations prevented export recovery and locked Haiti into persistent underdevelopment through a form of financial colonialism.
